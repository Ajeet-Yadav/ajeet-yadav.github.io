I am Rishabh Jangir, a final year undergraduate B.Tech student at Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati majoring in Physics. Research Intern at Robotics Research Center, IIIT Hyderabad. Former Secretary at Robotics Club, IIT Guwahati. Roboticist, tech-savvy innovator, excited by electrons.

Featured pages: Research page | DIY Robot hacks | Curriculum Vitae

My major contributions and interests are in the application oriented extensions of robotics, artificial intelligence and computer vision. I have a personal motivation towards soft reconfigurable robotics and internet of things. I am actively involved in the Robotics Research Center of IIIT Hyderabad as a Researcher under Dr. K Madhava Krishna. I am doing research in my bachelors' thesis project under Dr. Prithwijit Guha, IIT Guwahati. As a researcher I have intensively worked on:

Reinforcement Learning Learning from Demonstration Inverse Reinforcement Learning
Visual navigation and SLAM Monocular SLAM systems Autonomous Aerial Vehicles
3D stereo reconstruction Visit Research page to read more.

My recent work has strengthened my belief towards artificial intelligence and its application in the field of robotics, I believe that some of the very challenging problems in robotics can be tackled through machine intelligence. My current research interests revolve around deep reinforcement learning, multi robot co-ordination and human-robot interaction.
Over the course of my undergraduate studies I squeezed in three internships, two internships in computer vision and robotics at the above mentioned lab and one internship in IoT and embedded systems at a startup, SimpleLabs, where I worked on wireless data transfer protocols and networks in bluetooth low energy.


One of the best experiences in my undergraduate college life has been my involvement with the Robotics Club, IIT Guwahati. I headed the chair as the Secretary, Robotics Club 2015-16, leading a team of over 50 undergraduate students to work on a multitude of projects throughout the year. Visit page to know more. During my term I was actively involved in conducting lectures and workshops in my college community and organizing various competitions.


On a side for fun I blog, tweet, take part in Hackathons and build robots! Don't forget to check out my DIY Robot hacks.
